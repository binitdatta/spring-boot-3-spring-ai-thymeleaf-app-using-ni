Research Foundations of Generative AI

The iceberg metaphor illustrates how visible breakthroughs in Generative AI are supported by decades of foundational research submerged beneath the surface.

1950s–1960s: Early AI & Perceptrons
  • • Alan Turing’s Turing Test concept for machine intelligence.
  • • Frank Rosenblatt’s Perceptron — first neural network concept.
  • • Symbolic AI and logic-based systems dominate early research.
1970s–1980s: AI Winters & Backpropagation
  • • Funding cuts and limited computing power led to “AI Winters”.
  • • 1986: Backpropagation reintroduced by Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams.
  • • Neural networks began showing promise for pattern recognition.
1990s–2000s: Statistical Models & NLP
  • • Shift towards probabilistic models (HMMs, Bayesian networks).
  • • Growth in NLP research: translation, speech recognition advances.
  • • Foundations for modern embeddings and deep learning laid.
2010s–Today: Deep Learning & Transformers
  • • Advances in GPU computing and availability of big data.
  • • 2017: Transformer architecture revolutionized NLP (Vaswani et al.).
  • • Generative AI boom: GPT, DALL·E, Stable Diffusion, etc.
