Natural Language Processing in Generative AI

Exploring how NLP techniques power the understanding and generation of human language in modern AI models.

What is NLP?
  • • Field of AI focused on understanding and generating human language.
  • • Bridges the gap between machine processing and human communication.
  • • Enables chatbots, translators, and voice assistants.
Tokenization & Text Representation
  • • Splits text into tokens (words, subwords, characters).
  • • Uses embeddings (word2vec, GloVe, transformers) for numerical representation.
  • • Allows models to capture semantic meaning of language.
Language Modeling
  • • Predicts next word or sequence given prior context.
  • • Foundation for generative tasks like chat, translation, summarization.
  • • Evolved from n-gram models to transformers (GPT, BERT).
Context & Attention Mechanisms
  • • Attention lets models focus on relevant parts of input text.
  • • Enables handling of long sequences and nuanced meaning.
  • • Crucial for transformer architectures in generative AI.
Applications in Generative AI
  • • Chatbots and virtual assistants (e.g., ChatGPT, Siri).
  • • Text generation: stories, articles, and code completion.
  • • Multimodal tasks: image captions, speech-to-text systems.
Challenges & Future
  • • Ambiguity and context sensitivity in natural language.
  • • Bias and fairness issues in training data.
  • • Future: more grounded and explainable NLP models.
